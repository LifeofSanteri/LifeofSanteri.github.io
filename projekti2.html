Helsinki Design Week

I created a practice poster for the 2022 Helsinki Design Week, which focused on architecture. Through this project, I gained valuable insights into the fundamentals and implementation techniques of graphic design. Here are the key learning experiences I'd like to share:

Significance of Typography

'

The poster's title was a central element of its attractiveness. I carefully selected a font that was in line with the style and ensured that the title stood out effectively. Through the appropriate use of typography, I strengthened the visual identity and message of the poster.

Clear Communication

Communicating essential information, such as the event's date and location, was of paramount importance. I structured the text clearly and balanced it to make sure crucial details were easily noticeable.

Technical Proficiency

The technical execution of graphic design was a critical part of the project. Skill and precision were required to apply perforations and cutouts correctly. The poster's clean appearance was crucial to its success.

Visual Appeal

I recognized the importance of making the poster visually appealing. A striking appearance attracts people's interest in the event and encourages them to participate.

Emphasis on Theme

I understood the necessity of emphasizing the event's theme visually. This was especially crucial when the theme was 'We Are Open' with a focus on architecture. I carefully selected an image that reflected the festival's theme and created a strong connection between architecture and openness.


Overall, this task opened my eyes to the versatility and impact of graphic design. I learned that visual design is a powerful tool for communication and can inspire people to take action. This project helped me refine my skills and gain a deeper understanding of the fundamental principles of graphic design.

Some Other Designs

Grocery store advertisement

I designed and created an advertisement using Adobe Illustrator. In this task, I strictly adhered to the provided template, including fonts, colors, and layout dimensions. The advertisement featured the store's logo, contact information, a headline, tilted text, a footer text, and the main image for the offer.

Furthermore, I selected and illustrated six different offers with product images, each including price and quantity details. I aimed to maintain the visual consistency of the advertisement with the provided template and ensured that the text and images were prominently displayed.

This provided valuable experience in advertisement design while adhering to a template and taught effective alignment with the brand image, as well as the ability to work with available elements.

Långholmen Archipelago Hotel campaign

I created a practice magazine advertisement for Långholmen Archipelago Hotel, aligning it with the 'LONG HOT SUMMER' campaign's ambiance and the hotel's brand. Throughout this exercise, I learned several key lessons.

In terms of image selection, I thoughtfully chose images that conveyed the summer archipelago atmosphere and showcased the hotel's offerings. I paid particular attention to selecting a fitting image to replace the 'O', ensuring it was visually appealing, informative, and still easily readable.

The color palette I selected harmonized with the campaign's visual identity, emphasizing the archipelago and the experience of boating.

For font choices, I opted for fonts that were consistent with the hotel's brand and highly legible. The body text font lent the advertisement a professional appearance and contributed to the clarity of the text.

When it came to text content, I made decisions regarding the amount of text to include in the advertisement, ensuring that it conveyed essential information about the hotel's services and contact details. I aimed to make the text both informative and engaging.

The layout and design were also pivotal. I maintained a visual balance between the image and text, guaranteeing that the advertisement looked appealing while delivering the desired information.
